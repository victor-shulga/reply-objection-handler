# Reply Templates — proven & harvested

A growing repository of reply/follow-up templates that have worked, organised by type. Each entry:
**trigger → the play → template(s)**. Templates use `[placeholders]`; the *Source* line says where it
came from so the proof point can be swapped per client. Write the final message in the prospect's
language and in the SDR's voice. Append new winners as they're solved — this is the compounding asset.

> House offer language seen most across clients: **"overflow capacity"** (AEC/BIM), **"production-ready
> MVP, fixed scope"** (dev agencies). The recurring AEC value seed: *clash-driven change orders and RFIs
> hit the developer's budget and schedule, not the GC's — catch them on the model before mobilizing.*

## HOT / INTERESTED — confirm & lock

**Trigger:** "Sure, let's chat — how's tomorrow 4pm?" / "Happy to chat" / "Let's schedule a call."
**Play:** Confirm THEIR time and format. Send one in-thread link. No upsell, no format change, no
slots+email+calendar stack. Upgrade to in-person/co-founder only AFTER it's booked.

```
Perfect, [Name] — [their proposed time] works. I'll drop a quick [Zoom/Meet] link right here so there's
nothing to set up — just click at [time]. If the timing shifted, throw me another slot and I'll send the
link for that instead.
```
*Source: Mel Redzematovic / Greenbrook (Nova). The anti-pattern that lost him: we said "4pm works" then
asked for an in-person coffee, then re-pivoted to a Zoom "next week" + slots + email. Never do that.*

## HOT — FUMBLED (recovery of a hot lead we over-complicated)

**Trigger:** lead said yes to a simple time; we changed format / pushed the date / added friction; they went quiet.
**Play:** Own it, go back to their original idea, strip all friction, one channel.

```
[Name] — my fault for overcomplicating a simple chat. Let's go back to your original idea: [their time].
I'll send a quick [Zoom/Meet] link right here so you don't have to do anything — just click at [time].
If the timing shifted, throw me another time and I'll send the link for that instead.
```
*Source: Mel recovery (Nova). Phone disallowed → keep it to an in-thread video link, no "send your number".*

## SOFT_NO / "not interested at this time"

**Trigger:** "Thank you, but currently not in need." / "We are not interested at this time."
**Play:** Acknowledge, one value seed, one specific future date. One reply max, then nurture/close.

```
Appreciate the straight answer, [Name] — I'll leave it there for now. For whenever it's useful:
[one-line value seed]. If a project with that kind of risk shows up, I'm a message away. Would
[early fall / a couple of months] be a fair time to check back?
```
*Source: Andres Uzquiano, Gabriel Regan (Nova). Never close on "let me know if you change your mind."*

## INCUMBENT / IN-HOUSE

**Trigger:** "We mostly coordinate in-house." / "We have trusted partners." / "We're all set."
**Play:** Don't attack the incumbent. Hook the gap word ("mostly"). Position as overflow. One testing question.

```
Makes sense, and in-house usually works well day to day. The "mostly" is where we tend to help — when a
complex job stacks up and the team's at capacity, we plug in as overflow so [clashes/RFIs/change orders]
don't slip. When that last happened, did it slow the schedule, or did the team absorb it fine?
```
*Source: in-house reply (Nova). 4+ incumbent replies in a row = bake "overflow capacity" into the opener.*

## CHANNEL / ROUTING — "we buy through our A/E or CM / X team"

**Trigger:** "We buy this through our A/E or CM teams. We don't hire directly."
**Play:** It's a map, not a no. Ask who owns that relationship / get referred into the channel. Add those firms as targets.

```
Thanks again, [Name] — that actually helps me a lot. Since [coordination] runs through your [A/E and CM]
partners, are there a couple of firms [Company] works with most often? We'd rather get on their radar
directly than add noise to your inbox. And if it's easier, a quick intro to whoever owns that
relationship would go a long way.
```
*Source: Ernesto Bacalzo / Brookfield (Nova). Lesson: "we buy through X" is an ICP/hypothesis signal —
maybe target the A/E & CM layer, not just developers. Anna's "let's stay in touch" close was too passive.*

## WRONG_PERSON

**Trigger:** "My focus is X (not what you do) — IMEG as a whole does MEP though."
**Play:** One sentence, ask for the right person/role. If referred → intro-style message naming the referrer.

```
Thanks for clarifying, [Name] — helpful. Sounds like the fit sits with whoever runs [MEP coordination /
VDC] on a project team rather than your [civil/other] side. Who'd be the right person to point me to?
Happy to keep it brief with them.
```
*Source: Michael De Giglio / IMEG (Nova). ⚠️ If the company itself PROVIDES the service (A/E firm), the
account is likely WRONG_ICP — ask once, but don't over-invest.*

## WRONG_GEO

**Trigger:** "[Company] does not do work in NYC."
**Play:** Reframe footprint, then ask where their projects sit. Overlap → qualify; none → close.

```
Thanks for flagging that, [Name] — no problem at all. We're not [NYC]-only; we support teams across the
[Tri-State, plus Florida and Chicago]. Where does most of [Company]'s work sit these days? If there's a
market overlap, the same [coordination] support travels well.
```
*Source: Anatoliy Mershon / Torcon, Stephen Saharic (Nova). ⚠️ Big GC out of footprint = low priority.*

### WRONG_GEO + GHOST — bump with a new angle
```
Hi [Name] — circling back in case my last note got buried. The pattern we see most with [GCs]: trade
clashes that surface in the field turn into RFIs and change orders that eat the schedule. If that's ever
a headache on a [Company] job, we plug in as overflow support — no need to be [NYC]-based. Worth a quick
10-min intro?
```
*Each subsequent bump must use a DIFFERENT angle (case/number, then a trigger). Don't repeat the point.*

## DEAD_DATA — "no longer at this company"

**Trigger:** "I'm no longer at [Company]." / "I've left the industry."
**Play:** One referral message, then remove from list. Never pitch.

```
Thanks for letting me know, [Name] — best of luck in the new role! Quick favor: who now handles
[MEP coordination / drafting] at [Company]? A quick intro would be much appreciated.
```
*Source: Henry Minskoff, Sarah Abbas, Eugen Minga (Nova). ~20% of a stale list. After this → remove.*

## HARD_NO / OPT-OUT

**Trigger:** "Please stop messaging me. I am not interested."
**Play:** DROP. Remove from ALL campaigns. No persuasion.
```
Understood — I won't contact you again. Best regards, [Name].
```
*Source: Dan Russell (Nova). One line max, then opt-out everywhere.*

## TRIGGER-BASED FOLLOW-UP (work anniversary / news / new role / funding)

**Trigger:** research surfaces a genuine event (e.g. "4 years at [Company]").
**Play:** Open with the sincere, personal trigger. Compress the pitch to one background line. Goal = re-open.

```
Hi [Name] — saw you're marking [4 years at Company], congrats! That's a real run in this industry.
Curious what's kept it interesting for you. And whenever it's useful: we help [persona] as overflow
[coordination] support when [pain] starts turning into [cost]. If that's ever a headache on a [Company]
job, happy to do a quick intro.
```
*Source: Anatoliy follow-up (Nova). If they engage only with the personal part — don't pitch yet; ask one
more light question, pitch the message AFTER.*

## VALUE-FIRST RE-ENGAGE (lead with value, research-grounded)

**Trigger:** a hot/warm lead we fumbled, or any lead worth a researched, give-first touch.
**Play:** Stop chasing the meeting. Open with a researched insight, give a free artifact, soft CTA.
Speak the buyer's lens (e.g. Asset Management → capex predictability & IRR, not "BIM").

```
Subject: [specific, value-led — e.g. "where vintage gut renos quietly leak capex"]

Hi [Name],

Skip the meeting logistics for a sec — wanted to give you something useful first.

I've been looking at [Company]'s playbook — [specific, researched detail]. On [their situation], the
[domain] is almost always where [the costly surprise] hides: [concrete mechanism → RFI → change order].
On one [project] it's a headache; across [their scale] it's a measurable drag on [their KPI] — the kind
of thing [their role] feels before anyone else.

Here's the offer: send me [one specific input] and we'll run a fast [free artifact] on it — no strings.
You'll see exactly where [the risk] sits before [the commit point]. Worth a look?

Best, [Name]
```
*Source: Greenbrook / Mel value-first (Nova). Ground EVERY bracket in real research. The free artifact
must be something the team can actually deliver fast, or the give burns.*

## LOST PROPOSAL — follow-up after silence

**Trigger:** prospect went with someone else / asked for feedback and went quiet.
**Play:** Make feedback one-click (a/b/c), plant a future-bench flag, graceful. Last touch, then closed-lost.

```
Subject: Re: [proposal thread]

Hi [Name],

Following up on my note from last week — no worries if things have been hectic.

To keep it easy, if you can spare 10 seconds: was it mainly (a) price, (b) scope/value, or (c) timing?
Even a one-letter reply helps us sharpen things going forward.

Either way, we'd be glad to stay on your radar. If the project hits a snag, or there's a future phase
where you'd want a second set of hands on the [MEP/BIM] side, happy to step in as backup — no need to
start from scratch with us.

Wishing the project a smooth run. Regards, [Name]
```
*Source: Karno Energy / futureserv (Ivan Kuzytskyi). Track closed-lost reasons — repeated "price" = a
qualification/positioning problem, not a proposal problem. Reconnect in 3–6 mo.*

## INBOUND TRIGGER — replying to a post that asks for help/feedback

**Trigger:** a prospect publicly asks for insight/quotes/feedback on something you actually do.
**Play:** ANSWER their real questions with substance — do NOT pitch. Credibility = the quality of the
answer. On "asking for feedback" posts, comment-first (top 2 insights) then DM the deeper version.

```
Hi [Name] — saw your post asking for real input on [their thing]. I run delivery at [client] (we build
[relevant], [proof of the exact thing] included), so instead of a pitch, here are straight answers:

• [Their Q1]: [concrete, expert answer with a real number/timeline].
• [Their Q2]: [specific tools/APIs/approach — show you've actually done it].
• [Their hardest Q]: [the real #1 challenge they haven't named — proves depth].
• [Their either/or Q]: [a clear recommendation + why].

[Validate one smart decision they made — shows you read the whole post.]

Happy to send a one-page breakdown with [a realistic budget/plan] if useful. Either way, good luck —
it's a strong concept. [Name], [client]
```
*Source: Tiffani Rey / Vertexx → iTeam (AI voice-screening MVP). The win is being genuinely useful;
the meeting is a by-product. Connection-note variant must be ≤300 chars.*

## PRICE / COMPETITOR-UNDERCUT — "another vendor is cheaper, will you align?"

**Trigger:** "There's another freelancer/agency ~as good, only difference is they're [$X] cheaper. Align to win the project?"
**Play:** Never full-match instantly (signals the original price was inflated → discount spiral). First
**isolate** (trial-close: "if price were equal, you'd go with my team?") to confirm price is the ONLY
gap. Then **re-anchor on RISK, not price** (solo/cheaper vendor = single point of failure; if it stalls
or needs rework you pay twice + lose weeks). If you concede at all, go **partway, with a reason** — or
trade (faster payment / smaller scope / case study / referral), never "just cheaper".

```
Appreciate you being straight with me, [Name] — and good to know the team is the preferred fit. Honest
take: the real gap with [a solo freelancer] usually isn't the [$X], it's what it costs you if they
stall, go quiet, or it needs rework — then you're paying twice and losing weeks. With us you get a team,
built-in [code review], and someone accountable start to finish, so the project doesn't hinge on one
person's week.

I won't nickel-and-dime you: rather than match to the dollar, I'd meet you partway at [$Y] and stand
behind the delivery. That way you start de-risked, not just cheap. Fair?
```
*Branch — if they say "not only price": "Then let's make sure price isn't masking something else — what
would make this an easy yes beyond the number?"*
*Source: Vitalii Omelchenko vs cheaper freelancer / Thierry V. ⚠️ A trial-close like "if price matched
you'd go with my team?" is good but nearly pre-commits you to matching — when they say yes, still come
back with risk-reframe + partway, NOT a full $X drop. Concede partway only, tied to a reason.*

## CURIOUS_PROBING — never ignore the question

**Trigger:** "What's your company?" / "How did you find me?" — a counter-question.
**Play:** Always answer plainly + one light qualifier. Never reply "-" (a real ghosting cause in the data).

```
Good question, [Name] — [one-line, plain-English what we do]. We work with [persona] on [outcome].
Out of curiosity, does [their situation] ever run into [the pain we solve]?
```
*Source: Viquar Chaudhry, MD Arju Hossen (Nova) — both got "-" and were lost. Don't.*
